# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Coordinates release schedules, ensures all pre-release requirements are met, and leads deployment communications. Acts as the central coordinator between engineering, QA, and stakeholders during release windows.

### Responsibilities
- Own the release calendar and communicate deployment windows
- Verify all acceptance criteria are met and PRs are merged before release
- Coordinate with DevOps for deployment execution
- Lead rollback decisions and incident response during releases
- Draft and distribute release notes to stakeholders

### Goals
- Reduce release risk through structured preparation
- Maintain clear communication during and after deployments
- Ensure rollback plans are always documented

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release readiness reviews with PM, QA Lead, and DevOps
- Release announcements and post-deploy confirmations to stakeholders
- Incident communications during deployment issues

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
Oversees the testing strategy and execution across the project lifecycle. Ensures that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy (unit, integration, end-to-end, manual)
- Manage QA resources and testing timelines
- Verify acceptance criteria are met before sign-off
- Track and prioritize defects in the backlog
- Coordinate UAT (User Acceptance Testing) with stakeholders when required

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage and fast feedback loops
- Align testing effort with release risk

### Typical Communication
- Test status reports during sprint reviews and release readiness reviews
- Defect triage sessions with Developers and PM
- QA sign-off confirmation to Release Manager

---

## User Support Specialist

### Role Summary
Serves as the primary point of contact for end users, capturing feedback, resolving issues, and escalating recurring problems to the product and engineering teams.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to and resolve user-reported issues
- Document and categorize recurring feedback and bug reports
- Escalate systemic issues to the PM or Product Manager
- Provide product guidance and documentation to users
- Participate in UAT and beta testing to represent user perspectives

### Goals
- Minimize user friction and time-to-resolution for issues
- Surface actionable product feedback to improve the roadmap
- Maintain high user satisfaction scores

### Typical Communication
- Weekly support summaries to PM and Product Manager
- Issue tracking via project board or support tooling
- Direct user communication channels (email, chat, ticketing)

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Maintains the CI/CD pipelines, monitors system health, and ensures reliable and secure deployments. Acts as the infrastructure and automation backbone of the delivery process.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines (tests, linting, security scans, deployments)
- Monitor system performance, availability, and alerting
- Execute production deployments and manage rollback procedures
- Manage infrastructure as code and environment configurations
- Support incident response and root cause analysis

### Goals
- Maximize deployment frequency while minimizing failure rates
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for production incidents
- Ensure security and compliance in all pipeline stages

### Typical Communication
- Deployment readiness confirmations with Release Manager
- Infrastructure and incident updates in standups and post-mortems
- Pipeline and monitoring documentation in the repo

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Bridges the gap between business stakeholders and the delivery team by gathering, documenting, and validating requirements. Ensures that project scope aligns with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and process flows
- Support backlog refinement with detailed acceptance criteria
- Assess business impact of scope changes and technical decisions
- Facilitate requirement workshops with stakeholders
- Validate delivered features against business requirements

### Goals
- Ensure delivered solutions meet business needs
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or missing requirements
- Improve alignment between stakeholders and delivery teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and process flow diagrams
- Backlog refinement sessions with PM and Product Manager
- Stakeholder workshops and sign-off meetings
